Welcome to my final project!

My final project will center around female authors that played an integral role in classical literature. Listed below will be a bullet point summary about each respective author, along with facts about their life and works. Click on the links above to explore more about their literature! There will be a summary for their best works, along with video resources and links to other websites for more information regarding the authors. This website is great for people that would like to learn more about classical literature, particularly work done by female authors in the West.

  • Virginia Woolf
    • Virginia Woolf was born on January 25, 1882, in London, England..
    • She came from an affluent and intellectual family and was raised in a privileged environment.
    • Woolf's childhood was marked by the death of her mother when she was just 13 years old, which had a profound impact on her mental health.
    • She was part of the influential Bloomsbury Group, a collective of artists, writers, and intellectuals in early 20th-century London.
    • Woolf was a pioneer of modernist literature and is known for her innovative writing style, stream-of-consciousness narratives, and exploration of the inner lives of her characters.
    • Her most famous works include novels such as "Mrs. Dalloway" (1925), "To the Lighthouse" (1927), and "Orlando" (1928).
    • She struggled with mental health issues throughout her life, including severe bouts of depression and anxiety.
    • In 1941, Woolf died by suicide by drowning herself in the River Ouse at the age of 59.
    • Despite her tragic end, Virginia Woolf's literary contributions have had a lasting impact on the development of modern literature and feminist theory.
    • Her works continue to be celebrated for their introspective exploration of consciousness, complex characterizations, and experimental narrative techniques.
    • Sylvia Plath
    • Sylvia Plath was born on October 27, 1932, in Boston, Massachusetts, USA.
    • She showed an early talent for writing and published her first poem at the age of eight.
    • Plath attended Smith College on a scholarship and excelled academically, winning several prestigious awards.
    • In 1955, she won a guest editorship at Mademoiselle magazine but suffered a severe emotional breakdown during her time there.
    • Plath was diagnosed with depression and underwent electroconvulsive therapy (ECT) as part of her treatment.
    • In 1956, Plath met the English poet Ted Hughes, and they married in 1956. They had two children together.
    • Plath's most famous work, her semi-autobiographical novel "The Bell Jar," was published in 1963 under the pseudonym Victoria Lucas.
    • Tragically, Sylvia Plath died by suicide on February 11, 1963, at the age of 30, shortly after separating from Ted Hughes.
    • Her posthumous collection "Ariel" was published in 1965 and is regarded as a significant contribution to modern poetry. Plath's work continues to have a profound influence on literature and feminist discourse.
  • Mary Oliver
    • Mary Oliver was an American poet born on September 10, 1935, in Maple Heights, Ohio.
    • She gained critical acclaim for her keen observation of the natural world and themes of spirituality in her poetry.
    • Oliver won numerous awards, including the Pulitzer Prize for Poetry in 1984, for her collection "American Primitive."
    • Known for her reclusive nature, Oliver found solace and inspiration in nature and spent much of her life in Cape Cod, Massachusetts.
    • She passed away on January 17, 2019, leaving behind a significant body of work that continues to resonate with readers and inspire a deep appreciation for the natural world.
